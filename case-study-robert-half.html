Robert half

Client: Robert Half | Category: UI/UX Design

Overview

The project entailed the re-design of Robert Half's navigation system, the goal being, to guide the user in the most efficient manner to the main sections of the website.

Objectives

After performing user testing with our users, we became aware that there were usability issues with the current navigation. The user was also having difficulty in finding the content they were searching for while on the site. These navigation problems became our two main objectives.

User Flow

We started by separating the two main users - Job Seekers and Employers, making content available depending on the type of user. Also in this stage, we were able to categorize and reduce the number of links for easier access to the content of our site.

Wireframes

Wireframes were created, to explore different menu options as possible solutions.

Results & Implementation

Our early testing clearly showed that 80% of our users used the main navigation to search for specific content and only relied heavily on the search field, prior to this menu redesign. Testing also showed that most of the time users did not browse, being that users are task oriented.

For this project I worked with the Design Sprint methodology, which lead us to perform testing from the first weeks, which helped us advance quickly through our various ideas and land on the best navigation solution.
